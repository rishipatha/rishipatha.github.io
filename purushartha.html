Purushartha: The Four Aims of Human Life

Key Sanskrit References on Purushartha

  • Manusmṛti (discussions on Dharma, Artha, Kāma, Moksha)
  • Mahābhārata (especially sections like Shānti Parva, etc.)
  • Various Dharmaśāstras
  • Kāma Sūtra (for Kāma)
  • Arthaśāstra (for Artha)

Purushartha refers to the four aims of human life in Indian tradition: Dharma (righteousness), Artha (wealth/prosperity), Kāma (pleasure/desire), and Moksha (liberation). Each goal has its unique place, and a balanced pursuit of all four is considered ideal.
